Being a college student often means living on a tight budget while juggling tuition, textbooks, housing, and daily expenses. Fortunately, your student status comes with one significant advantage: access to exclusive discounts that can help stretch your dollars further. From software and streaming services to clothing and food, numerous apps and websites offer substantial savings specifically for students.

In this comprehensive guide, we'll explore 15 essential student discount platforms that every college student should know about, along with the specific savings you can expect from each.

## Why Student Discounts Matter

Before diving into our list, it's worth understanding why companies offer student discounts. Businesses recognize that students represent future customers with potentially higher earning power. By offering discounts now, they're building brand loyalty that can last well beyond graduation. For students, these discounts can translate to hundreds or even thousands of dollars in savings each year.

## Top 15 Student Discount Apps and Websites

### 1. UNiDAYS

**Savings: 10-50% off major brands**

UNiDAYS is one of the most comprehensive student discount platforms, offering verified student discounts from over 800 brands. Popular discounts include 10% off Nike, 15% off ASOS, and up to 50% off select items from various retailers. The platform also features exclusive deals that aren't available elsewhere.

To access UNiDAYS, you'll need to verify your student status using your .edu email address or student ID. The verification process is quick, and once approved, you'll have access to discounts across fashion, tech, food, and entertainment categories.

### 2. Student Beans

**Savings: 5-60% off thousands of brands**

Student Beans boasts partnerships with over 10,000 brands worldwide, making it one of the largest student discount networks. Notable offers include 10% off Adidas, 15% off Spotify Premium, and up to 60% off selected fashion items from brands like Urban Outfitters.

The platform's standout feature is its mobile app, which uses location services to show nearby participating restaurants and stores. This makes it particularly useful for finding last-minute deals while you're out and about.

### 3. SheerID Student Discounts

**Savings: 10-40% off verified purchases**

SheerID powers the verification system for many major brands' direct student discount programs. Companies like Nike (10% off), Adobe (60% off Creative Cloud), and Spotify (50% off Premium) use SheerID to verify student eligibility.

What sets SheerID apart is its instant verification process. Instead of waiting for manual approval, the system can verify your student status in real-time using your school's database, allowing immediate access to discounts.

### 4. Amazon Prime Student

**Savings: $70+ annually plus exclusive deals**

Amazon Prime Student offers a six-month free trial followed by 50% off the regular Prime membership fee. This translates to $69 in annual savings compared to the standard Prime membership. Additionally, students get access to exclusive deals during events like Prime Day, often saving an additional 10-30% on electronics, textbooks, and dorm essentials.

The membership includes free two-day shipping, Prime Video, Prime Music, and unlimited photo storage – services that would cost significantly more if purchased separately.

### 5. Spotify Premium Student

**Savings: $60 annually**

Spotify's student discount reduces the Premium subscription from $9.99 to $4.99 per month, saving students $60 per year. The student plan includes all Premium features: ad-free music, offline listening, and unlimited skips.

Even better, the student subscription often includes access to Hulu and Showtime at no additional cost, making it an exceptional entertainment value package.

### 6. Adobe Creative Cloud Student Discount

**Savings: Over $300 annually**

Adobe offers a substantial 60% discount on Creative Cloud for students and teachers. The student plan costs $19.99 per month (when paid annually) compared to the regular price of $52.99 per month, resulting in annual savings of approximately $396.

This discount provides access to the entire Creative Cloud suite, including Photoshop, Illustrator, InDesign, Premiere Pro, and more – essential tools for students in design, media, and marketing programs.

### 7. Microsoft Office 365 Education

**Savings: $69.99 annually**

Many students can access Microsoft Office 365 Education completely free through their schools. For those who need to purchase it independently, the student discount reduces the cost significantly. The education version includes Word, Excel, PowerPoint, OneNote, and 1TB of OneDrive storage.

Some schools provide this as part of tuition, but even when purchased separately, students save the full retail price of $69.99 per year.

### 8. GitHub Student Developer Pack

**Savings: Over $200,000 in developer tools**

GitHub's Student Developer Pack is perhaps the most valuable offer for computer science and engineering students. The pack includes free access to dozens of premium developer tools and services normally costing thousands of dollars.

Key inclusions are: free GitHub Pro ($48 value), $50 AWS credits, free domain names from Namecheap ($8.88 value), and access to premium IDEs and development platforms. The total value often exceeds $200,000 over the course of study.

### 9. Nike Student Discount

**Savings: 10% off all purchases**

Nike offers a straightforward 10% student discount on all products through their website and app. This discount applies to regular-priced items and can be combined with seasonal sales for additional savings.

For students who are active or need athletic wear for sports and fitness, this 10% discount can result in significant savings throughout the year, especially when purchasing higher-priced items like running shoes or specialized sports equipment.

### 10. ASOS Student Discount

**Savings: 10% year-round plus exclusive sales**

ASOS provides a consistent 10% student discount on their entire catalog, plus access to student-only sale events that can offer up to 70% off selected items. ASOS is particularly popular among college students for its trendy, affordable fashion and frequent new arrivals.

The platform also offers free shipping on orders over $35, and the student discount applies to already-reduced sale items, maximizing potential savings.

### 11. Headspace Student Discount

**Savings: Over $40 annually**

Mental health and stress management are crucial for student success, making Headspace's student discount particularly valuable. The meditation app offers students a significant discount, reducing the annual subscription from $69.99 to approximately $25, saving over $40 per year.

The student subscription includes access to hundreds of guided meditations, sleep stories, and mindfulness exercises specifically designed to help with study stress and academic pressure.

### 12. Grammarly Premium Student Discount

**Savings: $60+ annually**

Grammarly offers students 20% off their Premium subscription, reducing the cost from $12 per month to approximately $9.60 per month. For students writing essays, research papers, and reports regularly, this tool can be invaluable for improving writing quality and catching errors.

The Premium version includes advanced grammar checking, plagiarism detection, and writing style suggestions – features particularly useful for academic writing.

### 13. Coursera Plus Student Discount

**Savings: $100+ annually**

Coursera offers students access to thousands of courses, certificates, and even degree programs at discounted rates. The Coursera Plus student subscription typically offers 50% or more off regular pricing, allowing access to courses from top universities and companies.

This is particularly valuable for students looking to gain additional skills, prepare for internships, or explore fields outside their major without the cost of additional tuition.

### 14. Canva Pro Student Discount

**Savings: $120 annually**

Canva Pro offers students a significant discount on their design platform, often providing the premium version free or at a substantial reduction from the regular $119.99 annual fee. This tool is invaluable for creating presentations, social media graphics, flyers, and other visual content for projects and organizations.

The Pro version includes access to premium templates, stock photos, and advanced design features that would otherwise require expensive design software.

### 15. Local Restaurant Apps and Delivery Services

**Savings: 15-25% off food delivery**

Many food delivery apps like DoorDash, Uber Eats, and Grubhub offer student discounts or partnerships with student discount platforms. These typically provide 10-25% off orders or reduced delivery fees.

Additionally, many local restaurants near college campuses offer student discounts when you show your student ID, often ranging from 10-20% off your total bill. Apps like Yik Yak or local campus apps often feature these deals.

## Tips for Maximizing Student Discounts

**Verify Early and Often**: Many discount platforms require verification of your student status. Set up accounts early in your academic career to ensure you don't miss out on time-sensitive deals.

**Stack Discounts When Possible**: Some platforms allow you to combine student discounts with other promotional offers, maximizing your savings.

**Follow Brands on Social Media**: Many companies announce exclusive student deals through their social media channels before advertising them elsewhere.

**Check Renewal Requirements**: Some student discounts require annual verification of your student status, so set reminders to maintain access.

**Budget for Annual Subscriptions**: Many software discounts offer better rates when paid annually rather than monthly, so plan your budget accordingly.

## Conclusion

Student discounts represent one of the most underutilized money-saving opportunities available to college students. From essential software and streaming services to clothing and food, these 15 platforms can help you save thousands of dollars throughout your academic career.

The key is to be proactive in seeking out and applying for these discounts. Take the time to verify your student status on these platforms early in your college journey, and make checking for student discounts a habit before any significant purchase.

Remember that your student status is temporary, so take full advantage of these exclusive offers while you can. The money you save can be redirected toward more important expenses like textbooks, housing, or building an emergency fund for post-graduation life.

Start exploring these platforms today, and watch your student budget stretch much further than you thought possible. Your future self – and your bank account – will thank you for the effort.
